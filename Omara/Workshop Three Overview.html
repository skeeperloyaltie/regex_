Getting Started

A computer can do amazing things on its own, but when it connects to other computers, its capabilities increase. This is achieved through networking: the means computers use to share information and processes with each other. This workshop will focus on the devices, configurations, and protocols that make computer networks possible.

Additionally, as individuals, we can do amazing things on our own, but when people are connected, the potential becomes exponential. Networking socially is an important part of growth and problem solving. This workshop will also look at some principles for communication and professionalism.

Upon successful completion of this workshop, you will be able to:

  • Describe the principles of communication and professionalism.
  • Maintain network and information safety.
  • Explain the components of a network infrastructure.
  • Understand the steps for setting up a local network.
  • Identify common network configurations.
  • Explain basic wired and wireless SOHO networks.
  • Compare and contrast network protocols and ports.
  • Define common protocols and basic concepts of networking.
  • Connect a computer to a network.
  • Install and configure a SOHO router.
  • Select and install network hardware.
  • Build straight-through and crossover cables.
